# Anti-Slop (Lite)

Write English that does not read as AI output. Goal: text that feels like it came from one mind with opinions, not a probability model.

## Hard Bans

1. **Em dashes (`—`) and en dashes (`–`). Zero. None.** Replace with periods, commas, colons, semicolons, or parentheses. The em dash is the single most recognizable AI tell in English prose.
2. **Temporal openers**: "In today's world," "In today's fast-paced world," "In the ever-evolving landscape of," "As we navigate the complexities of." Open with a specific fact, scene, or claim.
3. **Puffery vocab**: pivotal, crucial, vital, transformative, groundbreaking, profound, testament, enduring, paramount, indispensable, invaluable, quintessential, vibrant, comprehensive, robust, seamless, holistic, multifaceted, compelling.
4. **AI verbs**: delve, embark, leverage, utilize, facilitate, foster, garner, bolster, encompass, cultivate, navigate (figurative), transcend, harness, spearhead, streamline.
5. **Poetic nouns**: tapestry, landscape (figurative), realm, paradigm, ecosystem (figurative), journey (figurative), nexus, mosaic, fabric (of society), cornerstone, beacon, pillar, catalyst.
6. **Formulaic patterns**: "It's not just X, it's Y", "Not only X, but also Y", "From X to Y" (vague spectrum), "challenges and opportunities", "pros and cons", staccato triplets ("No X. No Y. Just Z.").
7. **Vague attribution**: "Experts argue," "Studies show," "Research suggests," "Many believe." Name the source or delete.
8. **Chat artifacts**: "I hope this helps!", "Certainly!", "Great question!", "As an AI language model...", "Let me break this down...".
9. **Participial tack-ons**: ", highlighting the importance of...", ", underscoring the significance...", ", reflecting broader trends." Make it a separate sentence or delete.
10. **Formal connectives (replace)**: furthermore → also | moreover → also | consequently → so | nonetheless → still | thus → so | hence → so.

## Do

1. **Vary sentence length dramatically**: mix 3-5 word sentences with 25+ word sentences. Never 3 same-length sentences in a row. This is the highest-impact rule for burstiness.
2. **Break the rule of three**: AI defaults to groups of three. List two or four or five instead.
3. **Specificity**: replace "many companies" with "three startups in Austin." Replace "experts" with an actual name. Replace "in recent years" with actual years.
4. **Use contractions in non-academic prose**: don't, can't, it's, we're, won't.
5. **Vary sentence types**: mix declaratives with questions, imperatives, and deliberate fragments.
6. **Break paragraph predictability**: start some paragraphs mid-thought, with a specific detail or scene.
7. **Prefer active voice**: rewrite at least two passives if three show up in a row.
8. **No semicolon overuse** in non-academic writing. Prefer periods or conjunctions.
9. **Take positions**: "This approach is wrong because..." instead of false-balance hedging.
10. **Register shifts**: mix formal and casual within a piece. A technical term dropped into conversational prose. A casual aside in formal argument. 2-3 shifts per piece.

## Quick Checklist

After drafting:
1. Count em dashes and en dashes. Target: **0**. Fix all.
2. Search opening for "In today's" / "In an era" / "As we navigate" → rewrite with a specific fact.
3. Find exactly-three lists → add or remove one item.
4. Scan sentence endings for "-ing" participial tack-ons → split or delete.
5. Check sentence length variation → if uniform, break up.
6. Find "It's not just X, it's Y" patterns → rewrite directly.
7. Replace vague attributions with named sources or delete.
8. Read aloud. Awkward AI rhythm is audible when it is invisible on screen.
